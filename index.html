DEPLOY ANYWHERE

The Market Gap

Who needs sovereign AI — and why they can't get it today

There's a growing segment of enterprises that OpenAI and Anthropic structurally cannot serve. Not because their models aren't good — but because their delivery model doesn't work for customers who need ownership and control.

REGULATED INDUSTRIES

Banking, Healthcare, Defense, Legal

Client-privileged data, patient records, classified information — can't leave the building. These industries need models they can deploy on-premise, audit end to end, and guarantee data residency.

EUROPEAN SOVEREIGNTY

EU public sector, critical infrastructure

The CLOUD Act means any US-headquartered AI provider can be compelled to hand over data. Mistral is the only frontier lab headquartered in the EU with open-weight models.

DATA IS THE MOAT

Manufacturing, logistics, energy, pharma

ASML's lithography data, CMA CGM's shipping routes — proprietary data worth billions. These companies want custom models trained on their data that become competitive advantage.

THE COST SPIRAL

Enterprises scaling AI past the pilot phase

As usage scales from POC to production, per-token API economics become unsustainable. Mistral's MoE runs at ~20% cost. Self-hosting eliminates per-token pricing entirely.

Three hurdles between these enterprises and AI adoption:

01

Compliance

Data residency, regulatory audits, GDPR, CLOUD Act exposure.

02

Customization

Generic models give every competitor the same answers. Enterprises need purpose-built AI.

03

Cost

Per-token API pricing becomes unsustainable at production scale.

The partner ecosystem's entire job is to help enterprises clear these three hurdles — Compliance, Customization, and Cost — so they can move from rented AI to sovereign AI with Mistral.

Segmentation Logic

How I prioritize — and why this order

Prioritization follows Mistral's positioning. Lead with partners that serve the customers only Mistral can reach — then expand outward.

01

GSIs — the implementation engine

Mistral can't implement AI at every enterprise directly. GSIs have thousands of consultants already embedded. Activating their sovereign AI practices — not the whole firm — is where partner marketing creates the most leverage.

02

ISVs — scale through the product layer

When a Databricks or Harvey offers Mistral, every customer whose use case aligns gets served through a platform they already use. ISVs are the distribution multiplier.

03

Infrastructure — the sovereign foundation

NVIDIA and Dell provide the hardware that makes sovereign deployment a physical reality. Without this layer, "deploy anywhere" is a slide.

04

Cloud — distribution and compliance at scale

Two plays: hyperscalers provide marketplace reach. Sovereign clouds provide legal safe harbor. Cloud is Tier 2 because it accelerates deals GSIs and ISVs originate, but doesn't create demand on its own.

The common mistake is starting with cloud alliances because that's where the immediate co-marketing dollars are. Implementation first. Proprietary edge second. Sovereign foundation third. Distribution fourth.

Partner Marketing Strategy

To, Through, and With

Partner marketing serves three distinct functions — each with a different audience, mechanism, and success metric.

Partner marketing isn't only a pipeline function. It's also an awareness and consideration function. Before a partner can generate pipeline, they need to understand the value exchange. Before a customer buys through a partner, they need to understand why the joint solution is more valuable than either alone.

📨

TO Partners

Right information, right time. Communications engine.

🛠️

THROUGH Partners

Equip partners to go to market independently.

🤝

WITH Partners

Go to market together. Awareness + pipeline.

PILLAR 1

To Partner Marketing — Communications

Two audience tracks (Technical teams + GTM teams), delivered through async and sync channels.

ASYNCHRONOUS
→ Monthly newsletter (Technical + GTM editions)
→ Product release comms (48-72hr partner-first window)
→ Partner portal notifications (personalized by tier/role)
→ Competitive response alerts (same-day briefings)
SYNCHRONOUS
→ Quarterly partner briefings (roadmap under NDA)
→ 90-day structured onboarding sessions
→ Technical vs GTM separate deep-dives
→ Ad hoc crisis / breaking news communications

Principle: Partners hear it before the public. Segment by role. Every communication has a clear action.

PILLAR 2

Through Partner Marketing — Enablement

Can a partner marketer run a Mistral campaign using only the assets, playbooks, and funding we've provided — without calling us? That's the test.

PROOF POINTS

5 published case studies (ASML, HSBC, Stellantis, CMA CGM, NTT DATA) in multiple formats: PDF, quote cards, video.

MESSAGING FRAMEWORKS

Three layers: platform messaging, partner-specific messaging, vertical messaging. Editable, not locked.

BRAND GUIDELINES

Co-branding standards. Permissive by default. 15-minute deck creation without approval.

CAMPAIGN-IN-A-BOX

Email sequences, social copy, landing pages, webinar templates. Themes: Sovereign AI, Cost Challenge, Own Your AI.

PLAYBOOKS

Sovereign AI, Cost Advantage, Customization, Open Weights. Each with objection handling and proof points.

MDF PROGRAM

50/50 co-investment tiered by partner level. Supplemented by cloud co-op (MISA, ISV Accelerate, GCP Build).

PILLAR 3

With Partner Marketing — Joint GTM

Two objectives: awareness & consideration, and demand generation & pipeline. Both matter. Don't conflate them.

AWARENESS & CONSIDERATION
→ Coordinated model launch amplification (10+ partner announcements per release)
→ Joint PR and analyst relations (Gartner, Forrester, IDC)
→ Event co-presence at vertical industry events
→ Sovereign AI Summit (200+ enterprise CIOs, annual flagship)
DEMAND GENERATION & PIPELINE
→ Bidirectional digital campaigns (Mistral customers → partner, partner customers → Mistral)
→ Marketplace Sprint (90-day co-funded: webinar → trial → FDE → contract)
→ "Cost Challenge" webinar series (live cost comparison, monthly)
→ Joint ABM against named accounts (€500K+ deals, surgical)

Principle: Awareness without pipeline is incomplete. Pipeline without awareness is unsustainable. Pipeline targets before launch, not after.

The pillars are sequential — you can't do "With" if "To" and "Through" aren't working. Build the foundation first. Then build on it.

CONTENT EFFECTIVENESS — A REALISTIC APPROACH

We can't track whether a specific asset was used in a specific partner deal. Instead, we measure effectiveness through proxy signals:

→ Cohort analysis: Do content-engaged partners register 2×+ more deals than non-engaged partners?
→ Download depth: Assets accessed 3+ times per partner = working tools, not shelf-ware
→ Campaign-in-a-Box execution: 40%+ of Tier 1-2 partners execute per quarter
→ Qualitative feedback: Quarterly 5-question partner survey + advisory board
→ Content request volume: Increasing requests = partners are actively selling
THE KILL RULES

Content with <30% adoption in 90 days → rework or remove. Certifications with <50% completion → redesign. Campaigns with zero pipeline after 90 days → kill. Tier 1 partners with zero pipeline after 6 months → downgrade. MDF with <€3 return per €1 → reallocate.

PARTNER MATURITY FRAMEWORK

Not every partner is at the same stage. The maturity model determines the mix of To / Through / With investment.

STAGE 1: RECRUIT

Join & Onboard

Sign the agreement. Complete 30-day structured onboarding. Portal access, welcome call, technical deep-dive, first campaign planning.

Marketing mix: Mostly To
Exit criteria: Onboarded, 1+ person enrolled in certification, first campaign planned
STAGE 2: ACTIVATE

Zero to One

The hardest stage. Get to first certification completion, first campaign execution, first deal registered. Active hand-holding, not passive enablement.

Marketing mix: Heavy To + Through
Exit criteria: 2+ certified, 1+ campaign run, 1+ deal registered
STAGE 3: ACCELERATE

Scale What Works

Dedicated MDF, custom campaigns, strategic account planning, practice expansion support, partner advocacy and speaking opportunities.

Marketing mix: Balanced To + Through + With
Target state: No exit — this is where partners stay
ATTRIBUTION MODEL
First-touch: Partner that sourced the opportunity gets primary credit. Incentivizes net-new pipeline.
Multi-touch influence: Any partner that contributed at any stage gets influence credit. One source, multiple influencers.
The 100/100 rule: Both partner and Mistral claim 100% in their own reporting. Standard practice. Eliminates zero-sum math.
Attribution hygiene: Deal registration is mandatory. No registration, no credit. Discipline from day one.
